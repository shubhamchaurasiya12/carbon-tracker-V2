Frequently Asked Questions

What is a carbon footprint? +

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases produced by human activities, usually measured in CO‚ÇÇ equivalents.

Why is it important to reduce it? +

Reducing carbon footprint helps fight climate change, protect ecosystems, and ensure a healthier planet for future generations.

How can I calculate my carbon footprint? +

You can use online carbon calculators that consider your travel, energy use, diet, and consumption habits.

What are the main sources of carbon emissions? +

Main sources include transportation, electricity usage, industrial processes, agriculture, and deforestation.

Can changing my diet reduce my footprint? +

Yes, eating less meat and processed food significantly reduces emissions associated with food production.

Is carbon offsetting effective? +

Carbon offsetting can help balance unavoidable emissions, but it's best used alongside direct reduction efforts.
